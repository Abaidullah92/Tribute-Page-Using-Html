Biography of Dr. Asrar Ahmad:

Dr. Asrar Ahmad (26 April 1932 – 14 April 2010) was a prominent Pakistani Islamic scholar, theologian, and thinker, known for his deep understanding of the Qur’an and his efforts to revive Islamic teachings in modern society. Born in Hisar, British India, he migrated to Pakistan after partition and initially trained as a medical doctor before dedicating his life to Islamic scholarship. He founded Tanzeem-e-Islami in 1975 to promote a Qur’an-centered approach to personal, social, and political life, and authored over 60 books, including the monumental Urdu commentary “Bayān-ul-Qur’ān.” Dr. Ahmed delivered thousands of lectures, emphasizing the integration of faith with practical life, and critiqued contemporary political systems from an Islamic perspective. His teachings continue to influence students, scholars, and followers worldwide, leaving a lasting legacy in Islamic thought and education.

Timeline of Dr. Asrar Ahmed:

  • 1932: Born in Hisar, Punjab (now in Haryana, India).
  • 1947: Migrates with his family to Pakistan after the independence of the country.
  • 1950: Moves to Lahore and begins studies at King Edward Medical University.
  • 1954: Graduates with an MBBS degree and starts practising medicine.
  • 1955–1956: Active in student and Islamic movements, including Islami Jamiat-e-Talaba and Jamaat-e-Islami.
  • 1957: Leaves Jamaat-e-Islami due to differences over political participation.
  • 1960s: Moves to Karachi University and earns a Master’s in Islamic Studies while his interest in the Qur’an deepens.
  • 1971: Gives up medical practice after performing Hajj and decides to devote his life fully to Islamic teaching and da‘wah.
  • 1972: Founds Anjuman Khuddam-ul-Qur’an to promote Qur’anic education and study circles.
  • 1975: Establishes Tanzeem-e-Islami, an organization aimed at Islamic revival and reform.
  • 1981: Awarded Sitara-i-Imtiaz by the Government of Pakistan for his services in religious instruction.
  • 1980–1990: Delivers lectures, conducts Qur’anic study groups, and writes extensively.
  • 1990: Publishes numerous books and Qur’anic commentary including Bayan-ul-Qur’an.
  • 2002: Steps down from leadership of Tanzeem-e-Islami due to declining health.
  • 2000: His televised lectures reach audiences around the world.
  • 2010: Passes away on 14 April in Lahore.
